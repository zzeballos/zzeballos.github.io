Pilot Project Support Materials

To support an upcoming asset valuation implementation pilot project, I was assigned to design a variety of visual materials relating to asset valuation and how it could help various public agencies manage their various assets. The deliverables I contributed to the project include a promotional flier to convince potential agencies to apply to the project, a quick start guide that provides context by simplifying their larger 200+ page guide, and an alternative short video explaining asset valuation to potential agencies.

I started by designing the flier and the quick start guide to break down what was important about the pilot project and asset valuation. The flyers were built in PowerPoint to allow other employees in the future to edit them further and collaborate. I was one of few employees with access to Adobe software so I was conscious of using more accessible software when possible. For example, I edited the “Calculation Steps” graphic in Adobe Photoshop because once I revised the original chart into a more easily read PNG, no one needed to work on it further.

Asset Value Video

Since potential applicants for the pilot project needed to know what asset valuation was and how it could improve their agency, my team and I decided we should create a video. Short videos are often more engaging than pure text so in addition to researching and designing the two fliers, I repurposed my research into a short two minute video to introduce the topic and give context to the additional text.

I used Adobe Premiere to animate text boxes to highlight my talking points similar to a PowerPoint presentation, but with related stock footage edited together in the background. I edited diagrams in Adobe Photoshop to explain more complicated concepts. I then cut up the diagram to allow the graphic to reveal specific sections one at a time as I mentioned them in the audio. Once I was finished editing the video, an improved narration audio was added based on my stand in voice-over.
